## When should I use @sotajs/ddd?

When you have domain logic that benefits from encapsulation. If your entities are
just bags of properties with getters/setters and all logic lives in services,
you have anemic models. `@sotajs/ddd` makes rich models cheap enough to be
the default.

You don't need it if:
- Your app is purely CRUD with no business rules
- Your entities have no behavior beyond property access
- You're using a framework that forces its own entity model (TypeORM)

## How does this compare to NestJS CQRS module?

NestJS CQRS gives you building blocks (CommandBus, EventBus, Saga) but doesn't
enforce domain model integrity. You still write anemic classes. `@sotajs/ddd`
gives you the domain layer — entities with protected state, invariants, actions.
You use it *with* NestJS, not instead of. See [NestJS guide](/docs/nestjs/).

## How does this compare to Zod alone?

Zod validates input. `@sotajs/ddd` uses your Zod schema as the entity's type system
and adds: auto-setters, frozen state, identity comparison, invariants, domain events.
Zod is the foundation — the library builds the house on top.

## How does this compare to class-validator + TypeORM?

Different philosophy. TypeORM entities are database rows with decorators.
`@sotajs/ddd` entities are domain objects with behavior. They don't know about
the database. You map between them explicitly — see [ORM Integration](/docs/orm-integration/).

## Does this replace my ORM?

No. `@sotajs/ddd` is your domain layer. Your ORM is your persistence layer.
You hydrate entities from database rows in the repository, and dehydrate
them back before saving. Clean separation, no magic mapping.

## Why not just use classes with private fields?

You can. `@sotajs/ddd` automates the boilerplate: freeze, auto-setters, identity,
invariants, events. A hand-rolled class with `#private` fields and getters works —
the library just makes it 10 lines instead of 80.

## Is this a framework?

No. Four functions: `createEntity`, `createAggregate`, `createValueObject`,
`createBrandedId`. No decorators, no DI container, no base classes to extend.
You import what you need and use it in your existing project.
